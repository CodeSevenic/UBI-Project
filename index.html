UBI partnership with Sanlam reaches significant new milestone

Sanlam acquires a shareholding in UBI financial services subsidiary

The 18-year long relationship between Ubuntu-Botho Investments (UBI) and the diversified financial services company Sanlam has reached a new milestone, with Sanlam agreeing to acquire a minority shareholding in a subsidiary of ARC Financial Services.

Once the transaction has been concluded, Sanlam will have a 25% shareholding in the subsidiary company while ARC Financial Services will retain a 75% majority shareholding.

The deal follows Sanlam’s 2018 announcement of a package of broad-based black economic empowerment (B-BBEE) transactions, which are intended to realise the Group’s South African growth strategy through enhanced B-BBEE.

Mr Paul Hanratty, Sanlam Group CEO, said that Sanlam is delighted to have concluded this transaction with Ubuntu-Botho Investments. “This transaction strengthens our long-term strategic partnership with Ubuntu-Botho and creates the opportunity for further growth. Sanlam is well positioned to serve a wide range of clients and through our partnership with Ubuntu-Botho we are continuing to create tremendous value for a very broad set of stakeholders across South African society.”

New CEO for rain

Telecommunications company, rain, is key investment for ARC

rain is currently one of the most important assets in the ARC investment portfolio, which means that UBI shareholders are indirect owners of the company and should have a keen interest in its leadership.

The company recently announced the current Group CEO, Willem Roos, will become a non-executive director on 1 March 2021, after completing his three-year contract. He will be succeeded by Brandon Leigh, the current Head of Strategy, as incoming CEO. There will be a six-month handover period to ensure a smooth transition.

Brandon, as an original founder, now assumes the CEO role. Brandon holds a degree in electrical engineering from the University of Cape Town. He previously founded Leaf Wireless and won the South African leg of EY’s Emerging Entrepreneur of the Year award.
